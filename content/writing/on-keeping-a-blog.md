---
title: "On keeping a blog"
date: 2026-03-20
---

Most personal blogs die within six months. The usual failure mode isn't lack of ideas — it's over-engineering the site itself. You spend a weekend picking a framework, another weekend choosing a theme, another one adding dark mode and analytics and newsletter signup, and by the time the infrastructure is "ready" you've lost the energy to write.

The antidote is radical simplicity. Plain HTML. No JavaScript. No build step you'll forget how to run in two years. Write in Markdown, push to main, let a static site generator handle the rest.

The best blog is one you actually write in.
